TOMATO CROP DISEASE MANAGEMENT — ANDHRA PRADESH EXTENSION GUIDE

Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
Early blight is one of the most common tomato diseases in the Chittoor and Madanapalle regions.
Symptoms: Brown circular spots with concentric rings ("target board" pattern) on older leaves. Yellow halos around spots. Leaves eventually yellow and fall off.
Cause: Fungal pathogen Alternaria solani, spreads during humid, warm weather (24–29°C).
Management:
- Remove and destroy infected leaves immediately.
- Apply Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/litre or Chlorothalonil 75% WP @ 2 g/litre as foliar spray.
- Repeat sprays every 7–10 days during high humidity periods.
- Practice 2–3 year crop rotation; avoid planting tomato after tomato.
- Use resistant varieties such as Arka Rakshak.

Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)
Late blight is a serious disease, particularly during the northeast monsoon (October–December).
Symptoms: Water-soaked, greyish-green lesions on leaves; white mould visible on undersides. Dark brown patches on stems and fruits.
Management:
- Spray Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) @ 2.5 g/litre at first sign.
- Follow up with Cymoxanil + Mancozeb @ 2 g/litre every 7 days.
- Ensure good drainage in the field; avoid overhead irrigation.
- Destroy infected plant debris promptly.

Leaf Curl Virus (Tomato Leaf Curl Virus — ToLCV)
Cause: Transmitted by whitefly (Bemisia tabaci).
Symptoms: Upward or downward curling of leaves, stunted plant growth, yellow margins.
Management:
- Control whitefly with Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3 ml/litre or Thiamethoxam 25 WG @ 0.3 g/litre.
- Use yellow sticky traps @ 10–12 per acre.
- Remove and burn infected plants to prevent spread.
- Plant virus-free seedlings from certified nurseries.

Bacterial Wilt (Ralstonia solanacearum)
Symptoms: Sudden wilting of the entire plant; vascular tissue turns brown. Squeeze the cut stem into water — milky bacterial ooze confirms the diagnosis.
Management:
- No effective chemical treatment once infected.
- Remove entire plant with roots and burn.
- Solarise soil for 4–6 weeks before replanting.
- Apply bleaching powder @ 20 kg/acre into soil before transplanting.

Nutrient Deficiency in Tomato
Nitrogen deficiency: Pale yellow leaves starting from older growth; stunted plants. Apply urea @ 10 kg/acre as top dressing.
Magnesium deficiency: Interveinal chlorosis (yellowing between veins on older leaves). Spray MgSO4 @ 10 g/litre.
Calcium deficiency: Blossom end rot (dark sunken patch at fruit base). Apply calcium nitrate foliar spray @ 5 g/litre; avoid water stress.
