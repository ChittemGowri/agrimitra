RAGI (FINGER MILLET) AND DRYLAND FARMING — MADANAPALLE REGION

Ragi (Eleusine coracana) is the primary subsistence crop of Madanapalle's dryland farmers.
Season: Kharif (June–July sowing, October harvest). Also grown as Rabi in some irrigated areas.

Seed Rate and Sowing
- Transplanted ragi: Sow in nursery @ 10 kg/acre; transplant 25-day seedlings at 22.5 × 10 cm spacing.
- Direct sown: 5 kg/acre, mixed with sand for uniform distribution.
- Line sowing improves yield by 15–20% vs broadcasting.

Fertiliser Recommendations
- Apply FYM (Farm Yard Manure) @ 5 tonnes/acre before ploughing.
- Basal dose: N:P:K = 40:20:20 kg/acre (use DAP + Urea + MOP).
- Top dressing: 20 kg Urea/acre at 30 days after transplanting.
- Zinc deficiency is common in Chittoor sandy soils — apply ZnSO4 @ 5 kg/acre.

Irrigation
- Ragi needs 350–400 mm of water over the season.
- Critical irrigation stages: tillering (20 days), ear head emergence (55 days), grain filling (75 days).
- Excess moisture at grain fill stage causes neck blast — avoid.

Blast Disease (Magnaporthe oryzae)
This is the most damaging ragi disease.
Symptoms: Spindle-shaped spots with grey centre and brown margin on leaves (leaf blast); rotten, empty ear heads (neck blast).
Management:
- Seed treatment with Tricyclazole 75% WP @ 2 g/kg seed.
- Foliar spray with Tricyclazole 75% WP @ 0.6 g/litre at tillering and ear head emergence.
- Avoid excess nitrogen — it promotes leaf blast.

MSP and Government Support
- Ragi is under Minimum Support Price (MSP) procurement.
- 2024–25 MSP: ₹3,846 per quintal. Farmers registered with AP state procurement agency can sell at MSP.
- Awareness note: Many farmers sell to traders below MSP because of transport and documentation hurdles. Joining local FPOs helps access MSP procurement.

Nutrition and Local Market
- Ragi is highly nutritious (calcium-rich) and fetches premium prices in health food markets.
- Local ragi flour, malt, and porridge mixes can be sold at 2–3x raw grain price through self-help groups.
