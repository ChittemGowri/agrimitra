INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) FOR ANDHRA PRADESH VEGETABLE CROPS

1. Cultural Control (first line of defence)
- Crop rotation: Never plant the same family of crops in the same field two seasons in a row. This breaks pest and disease cycles.
- Sanitation: Remove crop debris after harvest and burn or compost it away from the field.
- Proper plant spacing: Crowded plants have poor air circulation and are more prone to fungal diseases.
- Trap crops: Plant marigold borders around tomato fields to repel thrips and whiteflies.

2. Biological Control
- Trichogramma egg parasitoids: Release @ 1.5 lakh cards/acre for fruit borer control (Helicoverpa armigera).
- NPV (Nucleopolyhedrosis virus): Spray 250 LE/acre for Helicoverpa control. Mix with 1 kg jaggery/acre as attractant.
- Neem-based pesticides (Azadirachtin 0.03% EC @ 3 ml/litre): Effective against mites, whiteflies, and soft-bodied pests.

3. Chemical Control — Safety Guidelines
- Always wear gloves, mask, and protective clothing when mixing and spraying.
- Spray early morning or evening — avoid midday spray when bees are active.
- Maintain Pre-Harvest Intervals (PHI): Imidacloprid (7 days), Chlorpyriphos (15 days), Profenophos (7 days).
- Never spray flowering crops with Imidacloprid or other systemic insecticides (bee toxicity risk).
- Rotate chemical groups to prevent resistance development.

4. Common Pest Identification
Fruit and Shoot Borer (Leucinodes orbonalis) — Brinjal:
- Dead shoots with entry holes; fruits with frass-filled tunnels.
- Spray Emamectin benzoate 5% SG @ 0.4 g/litre or Chlorfenapyr @ 2 ml/litre.

Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci):
- Yellowing, sticky leaves; sooty mould. Vector for leaf curl virus.
- Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3 ml/litre OR Spiromesifen 22.9% SC @ 0.75 ml/litre.

Red Spider Mite (Tetranychus urticae):
- Bronze discolouration of leaves; fine webbing on undersides.
- Dicofol 18.5% EC @ 2.5 ml/litre or Hexythiazox 5.45% EC @ 1 ml/litre.

5. Irrigation and Water Management
- Drip irrigation reduces foliar disease incidence vs flood irrigation.
- Avoid irrigation in the evening — wet foliage overnight promotes fungal growth.
- Deficit irrigation during reproductive stage reduces fruit quality. Maintain consistent soil moisture.
