GENERAL INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) PRINCIPLES FOR SMALLHOLDER FARMS

1. Monitor before you spray: Walk the field weekly and check the
   underside of leaves. Many pest/disease problems are caught early
   enough to manage culturally (no chemicals needed) if seen in time.

2. Economic threshold: Not every pest sighting requires treatment.
   Spraying only when pest/disease levels are likely to cause real
   yield loss saves money and protects beneficial insects.

3. Rotate chemical groups: Using the same fungicide/insecticide
   repeatedly leads to resistance. Alternate between different
   chemical classes (consult the product label's mode-of-action group).

4. Border/trap crops: Planting marigold around vegetable plots or
   maize around groundnut can intercept and reduce certain pest
   pressures naturally.

5. Water management: Most fungal diseases (blight, leaf spot,
   anthracnose) are worsened by prolonged leaf wetness. Morning
   irrigation and drip systems (vs. overhead sprinkler) significantly
   reduce disease pressure.

6. Local advisory: Andhra Pradesh's Rythu Bharosa Kendras (RBKs) and
   nearby Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) issue weekly pest/disease
   advisories specific to the district and season -- always cross-check
   any general guidance against the current local bulletin before
   large-scale spraying decisions.
