GROUNDNUT TIKKA / LEAF SPOT DISEASE — Management Guide

Symptoms: Two forms common in Andhra Pradesh -- early leaf spot (light
brown circular spots) and late leaf spot (darker, near-black spots
with yellow halo), both causing premature defoliation and yield loss
of 20-50% if untreated.

Cultural control:
- Use certified disease-free seed; treat seed with Trichoderma viride
  (4g/kg seed) before sowing.
- Follow 2-3 year crop rotation with cereals (maize, sorghum) to break
  the disease cycle in soil.
- Deep summer ploughing to expose and destroy fungal spores surviving
  in crop residue.
- Timely sowing (avoid late sowing) reduces disease pressure, as late-
  sown crops face spot disease during peak humidity periods.

Chemical/biological control:
- First spray of Hexaconazole (0.1%) or Chlorothalonil at 30-35 days
  after sowing, repeated at 15-day intervals up to 3-4 sprays.
- Carbendazim + Mancozeb combination products are effective against
  both early and late leaf spot when alternated to avoid resistance.
- Neem seed kernel extract (5%) as a softer alternative for low-to-
  moderate infestations.

Regional note: Chittoor district groundnut growers should coordinate
spray timing with the local KVK's pest/disease advisory bulletin, as
timing relative to monsoon onset significantly affects efficacy.
