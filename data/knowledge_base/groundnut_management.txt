GROUNDNUT CROP MANAGEMENT — CHITTOOR DISTRICT EXTENSION GUIDE

Groundnut (Arachis hypogaea) is the most important oilseed crop in the Madanapalle area.
Sowing season: Kharif (June–July) and Rabi (November–December).
Recommended varieties: K-6, Kadiri-6, Dharani, TCGS-1000.

Tikka Disease (Early & Late Leaf Spot)
Cause: Cercospora arachidicola (early) and Phaeoisariopsis personata (late).
Symptoms: Circular brown spots with yellow halos on leaves; heavy defoliation reduces yield significantly.
Management:
- Spray Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/litre at first sign of symptoms (around 25–30 days after sowing).
- Repeat every 10–12 days. At least 3–4 sprays needed.
- Tebuconazole 25.9% EC @ 1 ml/litre is effective for severe infection.
- Collect and destroy fallen leaves.

Stem and Pod Rot (Sclerotium rolfsii)
Symptoms: White cottony mycelium with mustard-sized sclerotia at soil level. Plants wilt and collapse.
Management:
- Treat seeds with Thiram 75 WP @ 3 g/kg seed before sowing.
- Drench soil with Carbendazim 50 WP @ 1 g/litre around base of plants.
- Maintain proper drainage; avoid waterlogging.

Peg/Pod Development
- At peg entry (35–40 days), maintain adequate soil moisture.
- Gypsum application @ 250 kg/acre at flowering helps pod formation (supplies calcium and sulphur).
- Avoid irrigation after 90 days to prevent pod shedding.

Harvesting and Post-Harvest
- Harvest when 70–75% of pods show dark inner wall colour.
- Thresh promptly; dry to 8–9% moisture before storage.
- Store in gunny bags in a cool, dry, ventilated room.
- Aflatoxin contamination risk is high if stored at >10% moisture — critical for export quality.

Groundnut Market Notes — Madanapalle
Madanapalle is a major groundnut trading centre in AP. Prices peak in May–June (lean season for new crop). Kharif crop arrival (Nov–Dec) causes price dip. Farmers with storage capacity benefit from holding 3–4 months post-harvest.
