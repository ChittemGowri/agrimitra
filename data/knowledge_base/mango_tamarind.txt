MANGO AND TAMARIND CULTIVATION — RAYALASEEMA REGION GUIDE

MANGO (Mangifera indica)
Major varieties in Chittoor district: Banganapalli (Safeda), Totapuri, Alphonso, Himayuddin.

Flowering and Fruit Set
- Mango flowers (December–January in Madanapalle belt). Cool, dry weather promotes flowering.
- Spray Paclobutrazol 23% SC @ 0.5 ml/litre on soil or foliar 4–6 months before expected flowering to induce early, heavy flowering.
- Apply Urea + KNO3 (1%+1%) foliar spray to boost fruit retention.

Common Diseases
Powdery Mildew (Oidium mangiferae):
- White powdery coating on flowers, young leaves, and fruits.
- Spray Wettable Sulphur 80% WP @ 3 g/litre OR Hexaconazole 5% SC @ 1 ml/litre at first sign and repeat after 15 days.
Anthracnose (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides):
- Black spots on leaves and fruits; post-harvest fruit rotting.
- Spray Carbendazim 50% WP @ 1 g/litre before flower opening and after fruit set.

Mango Harvesting and Marketing
- Totapuri and Banganapalli: harvest May–June. Peak price often in April before new crop floods market.
- Do not harvest immature fruit — causes poor storage and low market price.
- Pre-cooling and waxing extend shelf life for transport to Bangalore or Chennai.

TAMARIND (Tamarindus indica)
Tamarind is widely grown on field bunds and as commercial plantations around Madanapalle.

Harvesting
- Pods ripen February–May. Ripe pods are brown, dry, and easily removable from tree.
- Shell and separate pulp from seeds. Dry the pulp to <15% moisture for storage.

Pest: Tamarind Leaf Webber (Taniva spp.)
- Caterpillars web together leaves and feed inside.
- Spray Chlorpyriphos 20% EC @ 2 ml/litre in June–July when infestation starts.

Post-Harvest Value Addition
- Tamarind paste, blocks, and powder command higher prices than raw pods.
- FPO (Farmer Producer Organisation) membership allows collective sale, reducing middlemen margins.
- Off-season stock (July–September) fetches best prices — farmers with drying and storage facilities can benefit.
