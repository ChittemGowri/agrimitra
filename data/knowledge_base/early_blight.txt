EARLY BLIGHT (Alternaria solani) IN TOMATO AND POTATO — Management Guide

Symptoms: Dark brown concentric "target-board" spots on older/lower
leaves first, surrounded by a yellow halo. Severe infection causes
defoliation and reduced yield. Favored by warm, humid weather with
intermittent rain.

Cultural control:
- Practice crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops (avoid tomato,
  potato, brinjal in the same field for at least 2 seasons).
- Remove and burn infected plant debris after harvest; the fungus
  survives in soil and crop residue.
- Stake/trellis plants to improve airflow and reduce leaf wetness
  duration.
- Avoid overhead irrigation in the evening; irrigate early morning so
  foliage dries during the day.

Chemical/biological control:
- Spray Mancozeb (0.25%) or Chlorothalonil at first sign of spotting,
  repeating every 10-12 days.
- For organic management, Bacillus subtilis-based biofungicides or
  copper oxychloride (0.3%) are effective preventive options.
- Begin preventive sprays at flowering stage in regions with a known
  history of early blight, rather than waiting for symptoms.

Nutrient management: Balanced potassium application improves plant
resistance; avoid excess nitrogen, which promotes lush, susceptible
foliage.
