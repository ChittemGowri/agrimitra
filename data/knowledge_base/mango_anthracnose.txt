MANGO ANTHRACNOSE (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides) — Management Guide

Symptoms: Black/dark brown irregular spots on leaves, flowers, and
fruit. On fruit, spots enlarge and sink during ripening, leading to
post-harvest rot. Major cause of flower/fruit drop in humid weather.

Cultural control:
- Prune and destroy infected twigs and dead wood after harvest to
  reduce fungal inoculum for the next flowering season.
- Maintain adequate spacing and canopy management for airflow,
  reducing the humid micro-climate the fungus needs.
- Avoid overhead/sprinkler irrigation during flowering and fruit
  development stages.

Chemical/biological control:
- Spray Carbendazim (0.1%) or Copper oxychloride (0.3%) at panicle
  emergence, repeating at 15-day intervals through fruit set.
- Pre-harvest spray of Mancozeb (0.25%) 15 days before harvest helps
  reduce post-harvest anthracnose development in storage/transit.
- Hot water treatment of harvested fruit (52°C for 5 minutes) reduces
  latent infection before it shows up during ripening.

Storage note: Anthracnose often stays latent in green fruit and only
appears during ripening -- so field-stage prevention matters more than
post-harvest treatment alone.
