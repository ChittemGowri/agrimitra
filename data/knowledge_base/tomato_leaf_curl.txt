TOMATO LEAF CURL VIRUS (ToLCV) — Management Guide

Symptoms: Upward curling of leaves, yellowing between veins, stunted
growth, reduced fruit size. Spread primarily by whitefly (Bemisia tabaci).

Cultural control:
- Use virus-free, certified nursery seedlings.
- Remove and destroy infected plants immediately (roguing) to prevent
  spread to healthy plants.
- Avoid planting tomato near cotton, okra, or cucurbits, which host
  the same whitefly vector.
- Maintain 60x45 cm spacing for good airflow, reducing humidity that
  favors whitefly buildup.

Chemical/biological control:
- Yellow sticky traps at 15-20 per acre to monitor and trap whiteflies.
- Spray neem oil (3%) or neem-based formulations at 7-10 day intervals
  as a preventive measure, especially in the first 30 days after
  transplanting.
- If whitefly population is high, use recommended systemic insecticides
  as per local Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) advisory; always follow
  label dosage and pre-harvest interval.

Resistant varieties: Several hybrid tomato varieties with ToLCV
tolerance are available through state agricultural universities;
consult your nearest KVK for region-specific recommendations.
